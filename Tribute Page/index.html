Igor Ivanovich Sikorsky

"Man with a propeller. One of the largest aircraft designers of the XX century."

Sikorsky and helicopter prototype.

Here's a time line of Igor Sikorsky life:

  • MAY 25, 1889 Igor Sikorsky was born in Kiev, Ukraine (at that time was part of the Russian Empire)
  • 1901 When he was 12 he made his first toy helicopter
  • 1913 He built the first four-engine plane
  • 1913 Immigrating to the United States
  • 1923 Sikorsky founded the Sikorsky Aircraft Corporation
  • 1939 Sikorsky designed and flew the Vought-Sikorsky VS-300
  • OCT 26, 1972 He died of old age at the age of 83

Igor Sikorsky did not support the revolution. For a short time he collaborated with the French invaders, and in February 1918 he left Russia forever. In 1923, he founded Sikorsky Aero Engineering (now Sikorsky Aircraft) in the United States. First, this company was engaged in the design of aircraft and seaplanes, and then rebuilt to create helicopters. The development of technical thought allowed Sikorsky to return to the realization of the idea, which he rejected in 1910 as impossible.

The first successful helicopter Sikorsky (S-43 aka VS-300) was presented to the public in 1939. In 1942, the Sikorsky R-4 helicopters were put into service with US and British aircraft. Later, other models of Sikorsky helicopters were added to the arsenal of the United States and its allies.

In the postwar years, Sikorsky's helicopters set a number of aviation records: the S-61 model was the first of the helicopters to make a transatlantic flight, and the S-65 - the first flight across the Pacific Ocean. Igor Sikorsky worked in his company until 1954. Then he transferred the affairs to his son and retired.
